The Child
Capable Learner

Children are full of potential and curiosity, growing at their own pace when supported with care and guidance. The Reggio Emilia approach views children as capable learners who construct knowledge through exploration and interaction.

In My Practice

I observe children's interests, provide open-ended materials, and encourage them to explore and express their ideas freely.

Image: Sketchbook I believe families are the roots of a plant — providing strength, identity, and the foundation children need to thrive and belong.
The Family
Roots of Strength

Strong relationships between families and educators create a sense of security and belonging for children. Collaboration with families supports children's development and learning.

In My Practice

I build respectful relationships with families, communicate regularly, and include their values and cultures in the learning environment.

Image: Guide A guide supports children without controlling. Educators observe, listen, and respond intentionally — shaped through reflection and dialogue.
The Educator
Guide & Nurturer

Educators are like gardeners who nurture and guide children's growth. Educators support learning by observing, listening, and responding to children's needs.

In My Practice

I guide children through encouragement, provide support when needed, and create opportunities that promote independence and confidence.

My Teaching Philosophy

I believe teaching and learning is like growing a garden. Just as plants grow over time with care, patience, and the right conditions, children develop through experiences, relationships, and guidance. Growth does not happen instantly but through observation, exploration, and continuous support.


This belief aligns with the Reggio Emilia approach, which views children as capable, curious, and active learners. Learning is seen as a process that develops through relationships, creativity, and meaningful interactions. As an educator, I support children by creating opportunities for exploration, encouraging their ideas, and guiding their development in a supportive environment.

✦  Closing Thought  ✦

"Learning is not about reaching a perfect outcome. It is about the growth, exploration, and meaningful experiences that happen along the way."

Brown, Benson McMullen & File (2019)  ·  How Does Learning Happen? Ontario's Pedagogy for the Early Years (2014)  ·  Delgado (2020)

Community connections provide children with a sense of belonging and diverse experiences. Learning is strengthened when children are connected to their environment and community.

The Environment
The Third Teacher

A well-designed environment supports creativity, exploration, and engagement. The environment plays a key role in influencing children's learning and inspiring curiosity.

In My Practice

Curriculum Model
Reggio Emilia Approach

I believe the Reggio Emilia approach reflects my philosophy of teaching and learning. This approach sees children as strong, capable, and full of potential, emphasizing learning through play, relationships, and inquiry.
